Development environments: Designed, implemented, and documented key parts of App Inventor for Android, an online drag-and-drop development environment for creating Android apps, while employed by Google and subsequently as a volunteer on the open source project (January 2009-present).

Social software: Contributed features to the Orkut social network, including a recommender system, and developed ad placements for social networks, Google (January 2004-January 2009).    Designed, built, and maintained the Systers-mailman extensions to the Systers email list, Anita Borg Institute for Women in Technology (2000-2004).

Software agents: Designed and developed software to automatically recognize insulting or abusive email, applying natural-language processing and machine-learning techniques, Microsoft Research (May 1995-September 1995).

Compilers: Helped design and implement App Inventor for Android, a visual programming environment enabling non-programmers to create applications for mobile phones, Google (January 2009-present). Combined novel instruction scheduling and register allocation for a highly-optimizing compiler, Microsoft Research (Summer 1993, February 1994-May 1995).  Developed compilers for a massively parallel computer, MIT (1989-1992).  Designed and added optimizations to production compiler for switch statement code generation, Microsoft (Summer 1988).

Architectural evaluation: Evaluated the massively-parallel MIT J-Machine and Thinking Machines CM-5, MIT (1989-1993).  Constructed a system to analyze instruction traces for instruction-level parallelism (Summer 1990) and built a microprocessor simulator (Summer 1989), Microsoft.
